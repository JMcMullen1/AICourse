How Artificial Intelligence Works

Explore the fundamental mechanisms behind AI systems and discover how machines learn from data to make intelligent predictions and decisions.

How Does AI Work?

At its core, artificial intelligence is about creating systems that can learn from data to make predictions or decisions. Rather than being explicitly programmed with rules for every possible scenario, AI systems analyze patterns in large amounts of data and use those patterns to make informed judgments about new, unseen information. This ability to learn and adapt is what makes AI so powerful and versatile.

How Machine Learning Works: Teaching a Computer to Recognise Cats

Imagine teaching a young child to recognise cats. You wouldn't give them a rulebook—you'd show them many examples until they learned to spot the patterns themselves. Machine learning works the same way.

1

Gather Training Data

Show the model thousands of cat images—tabbies, Persians, kittens, and more. Each image is labelled "cat" or "not cat" to help the model learn.

2

Discover Patterns

The model starts identifying common features: pointy ears, whiskers, fur texture, eye shape—patterns that distinguish cats from other animals.

3

Train Through Iteration

The neural network makes guesses, receives feedback, and adjusts its weights millions of times. Each layer learns to detect edges, shapes, and complex features.

4

Make Predictions

After training, show the model a brand new cat photo it's never seen—it can now confidently recognise it as a cat based on learned patterns.

This Process is Called Machine Learning

Just as a child learns to recognise cats by seeing many examples, AI systems use machine learning to discover patterns in data without being explicitly programmed. The more quality examples provided, the better the model becomes at making accurate predictions. This same approach powers everything from facial recognition to language translation to medical diagnosis.
