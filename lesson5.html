Ethics & Oversight

Explore the critical importance of examining bias in AI systems and understanding how GDPR protects personal data and privacy rights in the digital age.

Bias and GDPR

As AI systems become more prevalent in our daily lives and business operations, understanding the ethical implications and regulatory frameworks becomes essential for responsible implementation.

Examining Bias in AI Systems

Examining bias in AI systems is crucial for ensuring fairness and equity in automated decision-making. AI models learn from historical data, which may contain existing societal biases — whether related to gender, race, age, or socioeconomic factors. When these biases are embedded in AI systems used for hiring, lending, healthcare, or project management, they can perpetuate and even amplify unfair treatment of certain groups. By actively identifying and addressing bias, organisations can build AI systems that make more equitable decisions and earn the trust of all stakeholders.

GDPR and Data Privacy

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is vital for protecting personal data and privacy rights in the digital age. As AI systems process vast amounts of personal information to deliver their capabilities, GDPR establishes clear rules about how this data must be collected, stored, and used. It gives individuals control over their personal data, including the right to know how their information is being processed and the right to have it deleted. For organisations deploying AI, GDPR compliance ensures they handle personal data responsibly, maintain transparency about automated decision-making, and respect individual privacy rights.

Key Considerations for AI Ethics

  • AI systems should be regularly audited for bias across different demographic groups
  • Transparency in AI decision-making helps build trust and enables accountability
  • Data used to train AI must be collected with proper consent and handled securely
  • Individuals have the right to understand and challenge automated decisions that affect them
  • Organisations must balance innovation with responsible data stewardship

Key Takeaway

Understanding bias and data privacy regulations like GDPR is not just about compliance — it's about building AI systems that are fair, transparent, and trustworthy. As AI continues to transform how we work and make decisions, thoughtful consideration of these ethical dimensions ensures that the benefits of AI are shared equitably while protecting individual rights. By engaging with these questions, you're developing the critical thinking skills needed to navigate the complex landscape of AI ethics in your professional practice.

Sustainability

The Hidden Cost of AI

Generative AI isn't "free." Behind every AI response lies a significant consumption of hardware, electricity, and water resources. Understanding these costs helps us make more sustainable choices when using AI technology.

Important AI Has Real-World Costs

Using generative AI like ChatGPT isn't without consequence — it has ongoing physical, financial, and environmental costs that are often invisible to users.

Every query you send triggers a complex chain of processes across large-scale data centre infrastructure. Your prompt travels to servers housing thousands of specialised GPUs, which consume substantial electricity to process your request. These processors generate significant heat, requiring extensive cooling systems that use both energy and water.

The financial cost of running AI services is substantial — from the initial billions invested in hardware to the ongoing operational expenses of electricity, cooling, maintenance, and staffing. While these costs are absorbed by service providers, they represent real resource consumption that impacts our environment and communities.

Tips for Reducing Unnecessary AI Use

  • Batch your questions — combine related queries into a single prompt
  • Avoid redundant prompts — refine your question before sending
  • Be specific and concise — clear prompts reduce back-and-forth
  • Consider alternatives — use search engines for simple lookups
  • Save useful responses — avoid re-asking the same questions
  • Question necessity — could this task be done without AI?

Politeness Has a Cost

Extra Tokens
Mean Extra Resources

Even small additions to prompts — like saying "please" and "thank you" — increase the number of tokens processed. While being polite is natural, it does consume additional computational resources with each request.

Every word counts

Sustainable AI Practices

  • Write clear, concise prompts to reduce unnecessary processing
  • Batch related queries together instead of making multiple separate requests
  • Consider whether AI is truly needed for simple tasks that could be done manually
  • Support and advocate for AI providers that use renewable energy sources
  • Be mindful of unnecessary follow-up queries — review and refine your initial prompt
